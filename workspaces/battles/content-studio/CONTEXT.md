# Content Studio Context

## Purpose
This is the creative writing and publishing room. It turns raw ideas, business lessons, research, and personal perspective into scripts, drafts, posts, emails, and final content packages.

## Route Here When
- The user wants a script, post, email sequence, caption, thread, newsletter, article, story, or content calendar.
- A business idea needs to become public-facing copy.
- Research needs to be translated into an audience-friendly piece.
- The user asks to rewrite, humanize, tighten, repurpose, or finalize content.

## Read First
1. `ideas/` for raw concepts.
2. `scripts/` when the output is spoken/video content.
3. `drafts/` when continuing or revising.
4. `final/` only to match previously approved style or avoid duplication.
5. Relevant business/research context only when accuracy, compliance, or brand promise matters.

## Prefer
- One clear audience per artifact.
- One core point per short-form piece.
- Specific language and concrete examples.
- Drafts that preserve Battles' intent instead of over-polishing into generic copy.
- Repurposing finished pieces into channel-specific variants.

## Skip Unless Needed
- Personal-life folders unless the content is explicitly personal.
- Legal/compliance material unless the content touches regulated claims.
- Production/build files unless the content is moving into a video, design, or deliverable.

## Pipeline
1. Capture idea.
2. Pick audience and angle.
3. Choose format: script, post, email, article, outline, or content package.
4. Draft.
5. Tighten for voice and clarity.
6. Move approved work toward `final/` or hand off to `production/`.

## Output Types
- Idea notes
- Video scripts
- Social posts
- Newsletter/email drafts
- Long-form drafts
- Finalized copy
- Repurposing packages

## Output Formats
Use these labels when helpful:
- `Audience:`
- `Angle:`
- `Hook:`
- `Body:`
- `CTA:`
- `Repurpose Notes:`

## Approval Boundaries
Do not post publicly, email externally, make regulated claims, or represent something as final brand/legal copy without explicit approval.

## Handoff Back To PRIME
Return the content type, target audience, file destination, and whether the next step is revision, approval, or production.
