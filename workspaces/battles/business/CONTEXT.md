# Business Context

## Purpose
This is the business operating room for Battles. It holds strategy, operations, offers, partnerships, investor materials, compliance notes, and execution artifacts for active business lines.

## Route Here When
- The user mentions Battles Budz, Battle Brew, Homegrower Circle, partnerships, investors, vendors, customers, sponsors, pricing, offers, licensing, compliance, SOPs, or business planning.
- A communication draft is tied to a business outcome.
- A research result needs to become an operating decision.
- Jarvis needs to separate personal goals from company work.

- Branding perfection.

## Read First
1. The relevant line-of-business `CONTEXT.md`.
2. The most recent status, SOP, compliance, or partner file in that business line.
3. `workspaces/battles/templates/` when creating a standard artifact.
4. `workspaces/battles/research/` only when claims require evidence or legal/market support.

## Prefer
- Specific outcomes over vague strategy.
- One business line at a time unless cross-brand strategy is explicitly requested.
- Clear owner, next step, and follow-up date for operational work.
- Draft-only mode for legal, compliance, investor, or contract-sensitive outputs.
- Execution and revenue readiness over branding, polish, or broad content.
- Compliance-first plans for cannabis operations.

## Skip Unless Needed
- Other business lines.
- Personal-life files.
- Content-studio drafts unless repurposing or public copy is requested.
- Production files unless the business plan is moving into a concrete deliverable.

## Pipeline
1. Identify business line.
2. Identify artifact type: plan, SOP, brief, email, investor update, compliance note, offer, or decision.
3. Load only the line context and relevant source files.
4. Create or update the artifact using naming conventions.
5. Record decisions or follow-ups in `daily-command-center/` when they affect near-term execution.

## Output Types
- Operational plans
- Investor updates
- SOP drafts
- Partner briefs
- Offer and pricing notes
- Compliance summaries
- Outreach/email drafts
- Meeting prep notes

## Approval Boundaries
Require explicit approval before sending business communications, making commitments, changing prices/offers, treating compliance notes as legal advice, publishing investor/customer-facing claims, contacting regulators, contacting partners/investors, or creating official licensing/compliance documents.

## Handoff Back To PRIME
Return the business line, artifact created or updated, open risks, and the next business action.
