## Purpose
This draft action plan focuses Jarvis on the shortest compliant path to revenue. It intentionally deprioritizes branding perfection, broad content, and community-building unless they directly support licensing, product readiness, distribution, or sales.

## Approval Rule
Jarvis must ask for explicit approval before external outreach, pricing decisions, funding conversations, partner commitments, contracts, purchases, regulatory communication, or launch decisions.

## Strategy
The current business priority is not "build everything." It is:
1. Clear final licensing/compliance blockers.
2. Confirm facility and production capability.
3. Pick the product with the fastest compliant route to first sale.
4. Secure only the partners/funding required to make that first sale possible.
5. Use early revenue to reduce pressure and fund the next step.

## 30-Day Execution Draft
This is a draft planning structure. Dates and owners need confirmation.

### Week 1: Truth Inventory
- Confirm current OCM/licensing stage.
- List official blockers and deadlines.
- Confirm facility status and inspection requirements.
- Gather compliance requirement/SOP list.
- Fill product readiness matrix with known facts and unknowns.

### Week 2: Readiness Build
- Draft missing readiness checklists and SOP skeletons.
- Build facility evidence binder.
- Identify required partners for product production.
- Estimate first-batch cost for each product.
- Choose likely first product based on speed, compliance, and cash.

### Week 3: Revenue Path
- Build retailer/distributor target list.
- Draft partner/outreach messages, but do not send without approval.
- Create pricing/margin draft, but do not finalize without approval.
- Identify funding gap only for first revenue path.

### Week 4: Execution Package
- Finalize internal draft readiness packet for review.
- Prepare approved outreach list.
- Prepare approved partner/funding asks if needed.
- Lock next 7-day execution plan.

## First Revenue Decision Filter
Choose the next action by asking:
1. Does this unblock licensing, compliance, facility readiness, production, or sales?
2. Does it reduce time to first compliant revenue?
3. Does it require spending, external communication, legal/compliance action, or a commitment?
4. If yes to approval-sensitive action, has Battles explicitly approved it?

## Minimum Viable Revenue Packet
Before outreach or launch, create:
- Licensing status summary.
- Compliance readiness summary.
- Product readiness matrix.
- Facility/production capability summary.
- Partner/distribution target list.
- Draft pricing/margin estimate.
- Draft outreach messages.
- Approval checklist.
