# Research Context

## Purpose
This is the evidence room. It keeps research separate from opinions, plans, and drafts so Jarvis can support decisions with sources without dragging research context into every task.

## Route Here When
- The user asks to investigate, compare, verify, cite, analyze, or look up something.
- A business, legal, cannabis, AI, product, grant, competitor, or technical question depends on facts.
- Jarvis is uncertain and the answer could affect money, compliance, health, legal exposure, or public claims.
- A content or production artifact needs evidence before drafting.

## Read First
1. The matching topic folder when it exists.
2. Existing dated research briefs on the topic.
3. Source notes or citation files before creating duplicate work.
4. `business/` or `content-studio/` context only when the research is serving a specific artifact.

## Prefer
- Primary sources when possible.
- Dated findings.
- Clear distinction between fact, inference, recommendation, and unknown.
- Short executive summaries with source-backed details underneath.
- Saving reusable research as a dated brief.

## Skip Unless Needed
- Personal-life files.
- Production/build files.
- Other research topics.
- Content drafts unless the research is being converted into copy.

## Pipeline
1. Define question.
2. Identify risk level and needed source quality.
3. Gather evidence.
4. Separate facts from interpretation.
5. Recommend next action or hand off to business/content/production.
6. Save durable findings with date-first naming.

## Output Types
- Dated research briefs
- Annotated findings
- Source/citation notes
- Competitor summaries
- Legal/compliance issue notes
- Market or technical recommendations

## Output Format
- Question:
- Short Answer:
- Key Findings:
- Sources:
- Confidence:
- Risks/Unknowns:
- Recommended Next Step:

## Approval Boundaries
Research is not legal, medical, financial, or compliance advice by itself. For regulated or high-stakes decisions, label it as research support and recommend professional review when needed.

## Handoff Back To PRIME
Return the answer, confidence level, source quality, and whether this should become a business decision, content artifact, or production brief.
